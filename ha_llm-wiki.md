### How Lucid Judgment Upgrades the LLM-Wiki Operations

**1. Ingest Phase – Calibrated Intake**  
Raw sources (papers, articles, ideas on heuristic algebra) land in the warehouse.  
Without Lucid Judgment: the LLM summarizes, updates pages, and links what feels relevant.  
With Lucid Judgment: the scaffold runs first. It demands proportional evidence before creating or updating any Markdown page. It flags weak signals, hunts for contradictions with existing axioms/operators, and only commits changes after asymmetric disconfirmation checks.

**2. Query Phase – Disciplined Synthesis**  
User asks: “How should we structure a Heuristic Algebra wiki?” or “Combine these two operators for trading signals.”  
The LLM searches the wiki, reads relevant pages (axioms.md, operators.md, scaffolds.md), then applies Lucid Judgment before answering.  
It generates a response only after:  
- Calibrating confidence to the strength of wiki evidence  
- Explicitly testing for disconfirming examples already in the wiki  
- Producing a clean, citable synthesis that itself becomes a new wiki page (e.g., lucid-judgment-applied-to-llm-wiki.md)  

Result: Answers are sharper, traceable, and automatically compound the wiki without noise.

**3. Lint Phase – Proactive Health Check**  
Periodic lint becomes a formal Lucid Judgment audit.  
The scaffold sweeps the entire wiki:  
- Are axiom pages still proportional to latest ingested sources?  
- Do cross-links hold under disconfirmation?  
- Is confidence calibration drifting (e.g., over-claiming on untested algebraic combinations)?  
It proposes targeted fixes, new questions to investigate, or algebraic recombinations to strengthen weak sections.  

Think of it as the warehouse manager walking the aisles with a strict checklist—fixing warped shelves and rerouting aisles before small issues cascade.
