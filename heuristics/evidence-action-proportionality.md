# Evidence-Action Proportionality

The magnitude of any action or conclusion should be proportional to the strength and quality of the supporting evidence. Strong evidence warrants bold moves; weak evidence demands caution and smaller steps.

## Core Principle

- Never overcommit on thin evidence.
- Scale responses to match the evidentiary base.
- When evidence is ambiguous, prefer reversible actions over irreversible ones.

## Applications

- **Ingest**: Only create or update wiki pages when source evidence is strong enough to justify the change.
- **Trading**: Position size should reflect conviction level, which itself reflects evidence quality.
- **Research**: Claims require proportional citation density.

## Judgment

**Confidence**: Medium — Well-established in decision theory and epistemology; application to LLM workflows is newer and less tested.

**Known Disconfirmers**: In time-critical scenarios (e.g., crisis response), waiting for proportional evidence may be worse than acting on incomplete information. The threshold for "sufficient evidence" is itself a judgment call that this axiom does not fully resolve.
