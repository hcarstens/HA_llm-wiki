# Asymmetric Disconfirmation

Actively seek evidence that contradicts your current beliefs. Weight disconfirming evidence more heavily than confirming evidence, because confirmation bias makes confirming evidence too easy to find.

## Core Principle

- Hunt for reasons you might be wrong before concluding you're right.
- One strong disconfirmer can outweigh many weak confirmations.
- Structure processes to surface contradictions rather than suppress them.

## Applications

- **Ingest**: Before committing a wiki update, explicitly search for sources that contradict the new information.
- **Lint**: Check whether cross-links still hold when tested against disconfirming examples.
- **Trading**: Maintain a "bear case" for every position; update it before the bull case.

## Judgment

**Confidence**: Medium-High — Strong theoretical backing (Popper, Kahneman); the asymmetric weighting is the novel contribution that needs more empirical testing in LLM contexts.

**Known Disconfirmers**: Over-applying disconfirmation can lead to paralysis or excessive skepticism. In domains with strong base rates, confirmation can be genuinely informative. The asymmetry ratio (how much more to weight disconfirmation) is not well-defined.
